What separates craft cocktails from everything else

The Algarve has thousands of bars. Most of them make the same drinks: a mojito from concentrate, a gin and tonic with whatever tonic is on deal, a Long Island for the stag party. That's fine. It's what it is.

The bars on this list operate differently. They start with the spirit and work backwards — what does this gin actually taste like, what does it want to be paired with, how do you make the best version of this drink rather than the fastest? At Forbidden Door, there are seven cocktails on the menu and the bartender can explain every decision in each one. At The Argo, the awards aren't decoration — they reflect genuine technique.

You pay more. The difference is real. If you've only had tourist-bar cocktails in Portugal, one drink at Forbidden Door will make you reconsider every drink you've had here.

FAQ

Is there a real speakeasy in the Algarve?
Yes. Forbidden Door in Lagos is the real thing — no sign, ring the bell, one room, serious cocktails. It's not a theme bar pretending to be a speakeasy.
What makes a craft cocktail bar different?
Fresh ingredients, made-to-order, bartenders who understand technique. The difference shows in the first sip. None of the bars on this list use pre-made mixes.
Are craft cocktails expensive in the Algarve?
Relative to a tourist beach bar, yes. Budget €12–18 per cocktail at the serious places. It's the same price as a mediocre mojito at a resort bar — except this one is actually good.
