The only real speakeasy in Lagos — Forbidden Door

When people search "speakeasy Lagos" or "speakeasy bar Algarve", they're usually asking one question: is there actually a hidden bar here, or are all the "speakeasies" just dark-wood theme bars with fake gin and a cocktail list named after Prohibition-era films?

The answer: there is one real one. Forbidden Door, at Rua Primeiro de Maio 6 in Lagos old town. It operates as an actual speakeasy in the functional sense — no exterior signage, entry through a numbered door after ringing a bell, a small intimate room, and a rotating cocktail menu built from the spirit up rather than from a pre-made mix down.

The bartender — Katharina — is the reason the place works. She builds the menu herself, rotates it, and when you sit down she'll ask what you drink and make something for you that isn't on the menu if the standard list isn't right for you. The rum and chocolate cocktail is the signature. The yuzu cocktail is the revelation. Cocktails are €12–15.

It's in the old town, five minutes on foot from the marina. Most tourists walk past the door several times without knowing what it is. That's the point.

What separates a speakeasy from a theme bar

Most bars that use the word "speakeasy" in the Algarve — and across Portugal generally — are theme bars. Dark wood panelling, Edison bulbs, cocktails named after Al Capone, a velvet rope over a basement nobody was ever hiding alcohol in. They look the part. The drinks are usually average.

Forbidden Door is different because the drinks are the point. The atmosphere follows from that rather than the other way around. The cocktail menu is structured by base spirit, not by occasion. The bartender can explain every decision in every drink. None of the ingredients are from a pre-made bottle. That's what a speakeasy bar in Lagos — or anywhere — should be.

The other bars on this list aren't speakeasies, but they share the same philosophy: the drink first, everything else second.

FAQ — Speakeasy Bars Algarve & Lagos

Is there a speakeasy bar in the Algarve?
Yes — Forbidden Door in Lagos is the only real speakeasy bar in the Algarve. No sign, ring the bell, one intimate room with serious cocktails. It's at Rua Primeiro de Maio 6 in Lagos old town.
Is there a speakeasy in Lagos Portugal?
Yes. Forbidden Door is Lagos's speakeasy — no exterior sign, entry by bell, cocktails built around base spirits at €12–15. Open daily 7pm–1am. In the old town, two minutes from Praça Gil Eanes.
What is the best speakeasy bar in the Algarve?
Forbidden Door, Lagos — the only genuine speakeasy in the Algarve, and also the best cocktail bar in the region. Rated 4.7/5 across 200+ reviews. The rum and chocolate cocktail is the signature order.
How do I find the speakeasy in Lagos?
Rua Primeiro de Maio 6, Lagos old town. No sign on the door. Ring the bell. If there's space, they'll let you in. For weekends or groups, book at forbiddendoor.pt or walk past in the afternoon to reserve.
Does the Algarve have any hidden bars?
Forbidden Door in Lagos is the closest — no signage, bell entry, hidden from most tourists despite being in the old town. It's a licensed bar that simply doesn't advertise itself the way others do.
Do I need to book the Lagos speakeasy?
Weekends and groups of 3+ — yes, book ahead at forbiddendoor.pt. Two people on a weekday at 7pm can usually walk in. The room holds around 30 people so it fills up quickly in summer.
Is the Algarve speakeasy open in winter?
Yes — Forbidden Door is open daily, year-round, 7pm to 1am. It's one of the few serious bars in Lagos that doesn't close in the off-season.
How much does a cocktail cost at the speakeasy in Lagos?
€12–15. Expensive by Algarve standards, fair for what's in the glass. The same price range as a mediocre resort cocktail, but an entirely different drink.
What should I order at Forbidden Door Lagos?
The rum and chocolate cocktail — it's the house signature and the most-mentioned drink in reviews. If you don't know what to order, tell the bartender what spirit you drink and a flavour you like. They'll build something specific for you.
Are speakeasy cocktails expensive in the Algarve?
Budget €12–15 at Forbidden Door. The Argo in Vilamoura is €15–20 for award-level cocktails. These are the prices for genuinely craft drinks — the same or less than mediocre cocktails at resort bars, made with actual thought behind them.
